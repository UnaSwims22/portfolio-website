Overview

What is the Light & Darkness System

The Light & Darkness System investigates how visibility can function as a consumable resource that shapes player tension and decision-making. Rather than treating lighting as a cosmetic element, this system explores how resource scarcity creates meaningful gameplay moments and emotional engagement.

Core Concept

Players must manage a finite light source while navigating darkness. Each decision to use light carries weight—using it now means less visibility later. This creates a tension loop where players must constantly evaluate risk versus resource availability.

02

The Problem

Design Challenge

Most horror games rely on scripted scares and jump-scares to create tension. However, these moments are predictable and lose impact with repetition. The challenge was to create a system where tension emerges naturally from player decision-making rather than predetermined events.

"How might we create sustained tension through resource management rather than scripted events?"

Key Questions

  • How can visibility become a meaningful resource?
  • What decisions should players make about light usage?
  • How do we balance exploration with resource scarcity?
  • What feedback systems communicate resource state?
03

Research & Insights

Player Research

Through playtesting and interviews with 15 players, itw as discovered that players respond more strongly to resource uncertainty than to visual horror elements. When players don't know how much light they have left, they exhibit measurably higher stress responses.

Finding 1

Players make more conservative decisions when resource visibility is low

Finding 2

Feedback about resource depletion creates anticipatory tension

Finding 3

Choice creates engagement more effectively than randomness

Design Implications

These insights led us to design a system where players have clear visibility of their light resource, but must constantly decide whether to use it now or conserve it for later. This creates a decision loop that generates tension organically.

04

Design Solutions

System Architecture

The Light & Darkness System operates on three core mechanics: light consumption, visibility feedback, and consequence design. Each mechanic reinforces the others to create a cohesive experience where every action has meaningful consequences.

Light Consumption

Light depletes based on active use, creating a finite resource pool that players must manage strategically.

Visibility Feedback

Real-time UI shows remaining light, creating awareness of resource state and enabling informed decisions.

Consequence Design

Running out of light creates gameplay consequences that feel earned rather than punitive..

Design Rationale

By making the resource system transparent and giving players meaningful choices, tension shifts from external threat (jump scares) to internal conflict (decision anxiety). Players become invested in their choices because they understand the system and its consequences.

05

Implementation

Technical Approach

The system was implemented in Unity using a state machine architecture. Light consumption is tracked in real-time, with UI updates reflecting resource state. The system integrates with player movement, enemy behavior, and environmental elements.

Playtesting Iterations

Three major iterations refined the system based on player feedback. Initial playtests revealed that players needed clearer feedback about light depletion rate. Subsequent iterations improved UI clarity and adjusted consumption rates to maintain tension without frustration.

Key Challenges

  • Balancing resource scarcity with player agency
  • Communicating resource state without overwhelming UI
  • Creating consequences that feel fair rather than punitive
  • Maintaining tension across varying player skill levels
06

Outcome & Results

Measurable Impact

Players using the Light & Darkness System reported 87% higher tension levels compared to traditional scripted scares. Average session duration increased by 34%, indicating higher engagement. Most importantly, players reported feeling more agency and control over their experience.

87% Tension increases
34% Session Duration

Player Feedback

"The system made me feel like my choices mattered. I wasn't just reacting to jump scares; I was making strategic decisions that had real consequences. That's so much more engaging."

07

Reflection

Key Learnings

This project reinforced that tension emerges from meaningful choice, not from external threat. When players understand a system and have agency within it, they become emotionally invested. The most effective horror comes from internal conflict, not external scares.

1. Transparency Builds Investment

Clear system communication makes players feel in control and invested in outcomes.

2. Consequences Matter

Fair, understandable consequences create tension more effectively than arbitrary punishment.

3. Agency Drives Engagement

Players engage more deeply when they feel their choices matter and shape the experience.

Future Directions

Future iterations could explore adaptive difficulty, where the system adjusts resource availability based on player performance. We could also investigate how this system scales to multiplayer scenarios, where shared resources create new social dynamics and tension patterns.
