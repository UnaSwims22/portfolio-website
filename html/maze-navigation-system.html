Overview

What is the Maze Navigation System

The Maze Navigation System is an exploration-focused system designed to create uncertainty through spatial design, visibility restrictions, and environmental guidance. Rather than directing players through explicit objectives, the system encourages players to construct their own understanding of the environment through observation and exploration. The system investigates how uncertainty can be maintained without causing frustration or disorientation.

Core Concept

The Maze Navigation System investigates how uncertainty can be used to create exploration without producing frustration. Traditional maze designs often rely on confusion as their primary challenge, but confusion frequently undermines meaningful decision-making. This system instead treats navigation as an information problem, where the player gradually builds understanding of the environment through observation, exploration, and environmental interpretation. The goal was not to create a maze that players could become lost in, but a maze that players could learn.

The Problem

Design Challenge

Maze environments naturally create uncertainty because players possess incomplete spatial information. However, excessive uncertainty can quickly become frustration if players lose the ability to form meaningful navigation strategies. The challenge was therefore to create uncertainty without creating disorientation.

How can a maze maintain uncertainty while still supporting player orientation and exploration?
03

Research & Insights

Player Research

Early playtests revealed a clear distinction between uncertainty and confusion. Players enjoyed not knowing what was around the next corner, but became frustrated when they could not understand where they had been or where they should explore next. This suggested that exploration becomes engaging when uncertainty is manageable rather than overwhelming.

Finding 1

Navigation is fundamentally about information acquisition.

Finding 2

Players do not navigate physical spaces directly; they navigate their understanding of those spaces.

Finding 3

This insight shifted development away from creating more complex layouts and towards designing better information systems.

Design Implications

The environment needed to provide enough information to support decision-making while withholding enough information to maintain tension. This led to a design focused on environmental communication rather than raw maze complexity.

04

Design Solutions

System Architecture

The navigation system operates by controlling information flow. Players begin with limited environmental knowledge and gradually construct a mental model of the space through exploration. The maze therefore functions as an information landscape. Every corridor, landmark, and environmental cue contributes to the player's understanding of the world. As players explore, uncertainty is reduced, confidence increases, and navigation becomes more deliberate.

Limited Visibility

Players can only perceive a portion of the environment at any given moment. This creates uncertainty about future routes while encouraging cautious exploration and environmental observation.

Mini-Map System

The mini-map provides partial spatial information that supports orientation without fully revealing the maze. It functions as a balancing mechanism, preventing uncertainty from becoming disorientation.

Environmental Landmarks

Unique visual elements act as navigation anchors, helping players construct mental maps of the environment and recognise previously explored locations.

Information Trigger Zones

Specific areas provide contextual information about objectives, hazards, or environmental conditions. These zones support gradual learning rather than direct instruction.

Design Rationale

The system was designed around the idea that uncertainty should encourage exploration rather than prevent it. Every navigation mechanic exists to regulate information rather than movement, ensuring that players remain curious about what lies ahead while retaining enough knowledge to make meaningful decisions.

05

Implementation

Technical Approach

The system combines level design, environmental storytelling, mini-map functionality, and trigger-based information delivery into a unified navigation framework. Rather than relying on objective markers, the implementation focuses on environmental communication. Players learn through observation, allowing navigation to emerge naturally from interaction with the environment.

Playtesting Iterations

Initial versions relied heavily on maze complexity, which often produced frustration. Subsequent iterations introduced mini-map support, but testing revealed that players became overly dependent on it. The final version balanced mini-map information with environmental landmarks, encouraging players to actively interpret the environment while maintaining orientation.

Key Challenges

  • Balancing freedom and guidance

Too much guidance reduced uncertainty and exploration, while too little guidance reduced confidence and engagement. Achieving the correct balance required multiple iterations focused on information delivery rather than level geometry.

06

Outcome & Results

Measurable Impact

Players reported higher levels of exploration, improved orientation, and lower frustration levels compared to earlier versions. The environment successfully encouraged curiosity while maintaining navigational clarity.

Player Feedback

Many players described the maze as feeling "uncertain but fair," suggesting that the system successfully balanced tension and comprehension.

07

Reflection

Key Learnings

The Maze Navigation System reinforced the idea that navigation systems are fundamentally communication systems. Players do not explore environments simply because they are large or complex; they explore because the environment continuously reveals meaningful information.

Future Directions

Future iterations could explore procedural generation, adaptive landmark placement, and dynamic environmental guidance systems to further investigate how information shapes exploration behaviour
